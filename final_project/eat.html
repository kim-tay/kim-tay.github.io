Simple Oatmeal Chocolate Chip Cookies

One of my favorite “mama” things to do was to have warm cookies ready for my boys when they got home from school. It’s nice to have a snack while doing your homework, don’t you think? I always hoped it made them feel like I was thinking about them when they were away. Most of my boys are grown now and I only have my littlest little coming home after school. I haven’t had cookies ready for him for some time, but I think I will do it today. These have always been our favorite as they are hearty and are easily adaptable by adding fruit or nuts if one wants.

Pre-heat oven to 350 degrees Combine in a medium bowl:
  • 2 cups flour
  • 2 cups oatmeal
  • 1/4 cup ground flax seed
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1/2 tsp salt
In a large bowl, blend until smooth:
  • 1 cup butter at room temperature
  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup white sugar
When smooth add:
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 tsp vanilla
Mix well.
Add dry ingredients to wet ingredients 1/3 at a time. Blend until mixed and add: 1 1/2 cups chocolate chips (adjust to your family's liking) I use a batter scoop (which is like a small ice cream scoop) to scoop dough onto cookie sheets.
Bake at 350 degrees for 10-12 minutes - depending on your oven. We have gas so I am not certain of the conversion to an electric oven~
